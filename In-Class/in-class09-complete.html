Q1: 95% Confidence interval for the overall score mean¶

$\bar{x} \pm t^* * \frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}$

where $s$ is the standard deviation of $x$.

Q2: Do minority and non-minority tenure-track professors get different ratings?¶

Hypotheses¶

  • $H_0$: The average rating for minority and non-minority TT professors is the same.
  • $H_A$: The average rating for minority and non-minority TT professors is different.

We have a sample of evaluations.

Conditions met¶

  • Independence: presumably they were sampled well.
  • Normality: n < 30, no clear outliers?, n > 30 no extreme outliers

Use probplot

In [12]:

In [13]:

Conclusion¶

The lower outlier is kind of extreme, but it would tend to pull the non-minority mean lower, so if we removed it, it would not change our conclusion. Similar for the light-tail to the right: fewer high values, so a really normal distribution would show a more extreme difference.

We can reject the null hypothesis with significance level $p < 0.05$.
